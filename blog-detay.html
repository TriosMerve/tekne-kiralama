Zurich is Switzerland’s largest city, consistently ranking high in global surveys for quality of life. With its stunning location, medieval history, and modern infrastructure, Zurich offers a unique combination of old-world charm and cutting-edge innovation. However, like any major city, living in Zurich has advantages and disadvantages.



Considering moving to Zurich soon? Whether for work, study, or a change of scenery, it’s important to weigh the pros and cons of relocating. This comprehensive guide will help you understand the key aspects of Zurich life, including the cost of living, cultural opportunities, and practical considerations.

Pros of living in Zurich in 2024



High quality of life

This is one of the biggest reasons Zurich is a top city in Europe. In 2024, the city continues to be celebrated for its clean environment, efficient public services, and excellent infrastructure.

Key highlights:
  • Cleanliness: Zurich is known for how pristine it is. Streets are well-maintained, public spaces are spotless, and littering is highly discouraged. The end result? A really inviting look and feel.
  • Public transportation: The city’s public transport system is world-class, with frequent trains, trams, and buses connecting every part of the city and the surrounding region. The system is punctual, reliable, and easy to navigate.
  • Safety: Zurich has a low crime rate, making it one of the safest cities in the world to live in. Plus, you can comfortably walk around most parts of the city at any time of day.

2. Strong economy & job market

Zurich is Switzerland’s financial hub. It’s home to major international banks, financial institutions, and multinational corporations. The city’s economy is diverse, with the finance, pharmaceuticals, technology, and engineering industries leading the way.
